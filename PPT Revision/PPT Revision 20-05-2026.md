
**Clipboard Group**
*   **Paste:** Inserts content you have previously copied or cut onto your slide.
*   **Cut (scissors icon):** Removes the selected text or object and temporarily saves it to your clipboard so you can paste it elsewhere.
*   **Copy (two pages icon):** Duplicates the selected item to your clipboard without removing the original.
*   **Format Painter (paintbrush icon):** Copies the formatting (like color, font, and size) from one piece of text or object and applies it to another.

**Slides Group**
*   **New Slide:** Adds a brand-new slide to your presentation. Clicking the bottom half of the button lets you choose a specific layout.
*   **Layout:** Changes the arrangement of placeholders (text boxes, image boxes) on the currently selected slide.
*   **Reset:** Reverts the size, position, and formatting of the slide's placeholders back to their default settings if you've moved them around.
*   **Section:** Allows you to organize a large presentation by grouping sets of slides into collapsible sections.

**Font Group**
*   **Font Face & Size (e.g., "Calibri Light (Headings)" and "60"):** Dropdown menus that let you change the style of your text and its exact numerical size.
*   **Increase/Decrease Font Size (A^ / A v):** Buttons to quickly bump the selected text size up or down.
*   **Clear All Formatting (A with an eraser):** Strips all styling (bold, colors, etc.) from the selected text, returning it to standard plain text.
*   **Bold (B), Italic (*I*), Underline (U):** Standard formatting tools to add emphasis to your words.
*   **Text Shadow (S):** Adds a subtle drop-shadow behind the text to help it stand out, especially against busy backgrounds.
*   **Strikethrough (ab):** Draws a line straight through the middle of the selected text.
*   **Character Spacing (AV↔):** Adjusts how tight or loose the horizontal spacing is between individual letters.
*   **Change Case (Aa):** A quick way to change selected text to UPPERCASE, lowercase, Sentence case, or Capitalize Each Word without retyping.
*   **Text Highlight Color (marker with yellow line):** Places a colored background right behind the text, mimicking a highlighter pen.
*   **Font Color (A with red line):** Changes the color of the text itself.
